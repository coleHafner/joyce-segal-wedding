Adventures in Seattle

Seattle City Sites

  • DOWNTOWN & PIONEER SQUARE
    • Pike Place Market - Great food, arts and crafts, people watching, and the original Starbucks (coffee is no different, but cool to see and usually has performers playing music!).
    • Underground Tour
    • Olympic Sculpture Garden
    • Seattle Library (1001 4th Ave S.) – The architecture of this place is worth a visit
    • Mariners baseball game at Safeco Field
    • Ferry to Bainbridge Island - The ferry terminal is a short walk from the Edgewater Hotel, ferry ride is about 15 minutes and the island is beautiful! Shops, good food, kayak rentals.
    • Seattle Art Museum (SAM) – Especially if the weather isn't cooperating.
    • Flagship REI Store (222 Yale Ave. N)
  • SEATTLE CENTER
    • Space Needle
    • Experience Music Project (EMP)
    • International Fountain – Festivals take place almost every weekend here throughout the summer. The weekend of the wedding has Indigenous Cultures Day (August 16th) and BrasilFest (August 17th).
    • Chihuly Glass Museum – Unique local glass artist.
  • BALLARD
    • Hiram M. Chittenden Locks
    • Golden Gardens
    • Discovery Park – 3-5 mile walking loop and trails. Great views of the Olympic mountains from the bluffs.
  • CAPITOL HILL
    • Volunteer Park and Conservatory – Climb the water tower and learn some Seattle history.
  • FREMONT
    • Walking tour – Frommer's does a pretty good job describing all the quirky sites! Stop at the Fremont Brewing Company and enjoy a family atmosphere of local brews. You can bring pets, kids, and your own food here! This is one of our favorite neighborhood digs!
  • HIKING
    • Snoqualmie Pass - (I-90 Corridor) often referred to as the Issaquah Alps offers an array of hikes just a 30-45 minute drive away. Check out the Washington Trails Association (WTA) sites for more details!
    • Exit 32 - Rattlesnake ledge/lake.
    • Exit 32 - Mt Si (or Little Si for a shorter hike!).
    • Exit 25 - Snoqualmie falls.
    • Exit 47 - Denny Creek Trail.
  • EATERIES (because good food matters)
    • Market Grill in Pike Place Market – The best fish sandwiches in town!
    • Michou Deli - Also a great option on the street adjacent to Pike Place. Lots of options and cheap prices!
    • Marination Ma Kai (1660 Harbor Ave SW) – The best Hawaiian grub outside of Hawaii! This one is located in West Seattle, but you can take a water taxi from downtown directly to the restaurant which is an adventure all in itself!
    • Etta's Seafood (2020 Western Ave.) – One of Tom Douglas' original restaurants at the end of Pike Place Market.
    • Dahlia Lounge (2001 4th Ave.) – Another Tom Douglas restaurant worth visiting.
    • Salumi (309 Third Ave S.) – Incredible deli sandwiches in Pioneer Square.
    • Macrina Bakery (2408 First Avenue) – This place is known for its brunches.
    • Agua Verde (1303 NE Boat St.) – Authentic Mexican food near the University of WA, off of Lake Union (rent a kayak here too and check out the house boats!).
    • Long Provincial Vietnamese (2nd Ave and Stewart)
    • Revel (Fremont, 403 N. 36th St.) - Korean fusion. Amazing and well worth the wait!
    • Paseo (Fremont, 4225 Fremont Ave. N; Ballard near the locks: 6226 Seaview Ave NW) – Cuban food to die for!
    • Ray's Boathouse (6049 Seaview Ave NW) – Sunsets and happy hour in Ballard near the Locks.
